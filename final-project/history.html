History of Pennies

This page briefly outlines the history of the design of the penny since its creation in 1787 until now. The pennies are broken up into 5 main design categories, and clicking the links to the left will take you to more information about the coins in each category.

Fugio Cent

1787-1788

Designer: Benjamin Franklin

Though not technically a penny, it was the first official United States circulation coin and was worth $0.01. The design for the coins was based on 1776 Continental Dollar coin, which was never released into circulation. The coins consisted of 10 g of copper. The 13 chain links on the reverse side represent the 13 original colonies. The “we are one” motto was replaced with “E pluribus unum” on later coins.

Flowing Hair Cents

1793

Designer: Henry Voigt

There are two designs of the Flowing Hair cents: the chain and the wreath. The 15 chain links represent the 15 states in existence at the time. Public reaction to the coin was fairly negative, as it was said the liberty head appeared to be “in a fright” and some interpreted the chain to be representative of slavery. The design of the liberty head was modified slightly to address criticism of the chain penny, resulting in the wreath penny.

Liberty Cap Cent

1773-1796

Designer: Joseph Wright

This coin pictures the goddess of liberty and her phrygian cap. The design was based on changes to satisy complaints from the public about the Flowing Hair pennies.
